## Description

**Bug Report — UAT Sprint 8**

The Policyholder 360 dashboard displays an inflated 'Open Claims' count when a policyholder (Account) has multiple Policy__c records that share the same TIN__c value. The count appears to multiply per policy record rather than deduplicating at the Claim__c level.

**Steps to Reproduce:**
1. Identify or create an Account with 3 Policy__c records all having Policy__c.TIN__c = '12-3456789'.
2. Create 2 Claim__c records linked to Policy__c #1, both with Claim__c.Status__c = 'Open'.
3. Create 1 Claim__c linked to Policy__c #2 with Status__c = 'Open'.
4. Navigate to the Account's Policyholder 360 Lightning page.
5. Observe the 'Open Claims' KPI tile.

**Expected Behavior:**
Open Claims count = 3 (distinct Claim__c records with Status__c = 'Open' across all policies for this account).

**Actual Behavior:**
Open Claims count = 9 (appears to be 3 claims × 3 policies sharing the same TIN — a cross-join artifact in the DataRaptor aggregation query).

**Environment:** Full Sandbox (ACME-FS01)
**First reported by:** Claims Operations Manager during UAT session 8.3

## Solution

Under investigation. Preliminary finding: the DataRaptor Extract 'DR_Get_Policyholder_OpenClaims' uses TIN__c as the join key across Policy__c and Claim__c, producing a Cartesian product when multiple policies share a TIN. Fix approach: rewrite the DataRaptor to join on Policy__c.Id → Claim__c.Policy__c (lookup field) and aggregate COUNT(DISTINCT Claim__c.Id) where Status__c = 'Open'. Alternatively, evaluate moving aggregation to an Apex @AuraEnabled method with a proper GROUP BY SOQL query. Fix in development — targeting deployment to sandbox by end of sprint.
