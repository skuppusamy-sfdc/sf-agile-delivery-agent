## Description

**Bug Report — Coverage History Timeline LWC (INS-0764)**

**Steps to Reproduce:**
1. Navigate to any Policy__c record where Policy__c.COB_Indicator__c = true and at least one Rider__c record has Rider__c.Rider_Type__c = 'COB'.
2. Open the Coverage History Timeline LWC on the Policy__c Lightning Record Page.
3. Observe the rendered timeline nodes.

**Expected Behavior:**
Each Coverage_Period__c record should appear exactly once in the timeline, regardless of the number of associated COB riders.

**Actual Behavior:**
Coverage_Period__c records are duplicated in the timeline — one entry per linked Rider__c record of type 'COB'. For example, a policy with 3 COB riders and 5 coverage periods renders 15 timeline nodes instead of 5.

**Environment:** Acme Insurance Full Sandbox (SF-FULL-02)
**Regression:** Introduced in Sprint 9 deployment on 2024-11-04
**Affected Records:** Confirmed on Policy__c IDs: a0B3x000001AbCd, a0B3x000001XyZw (anonymized)

**Root Cause Hypothesis:**
The SOQL subquery on Rider__c within CoverageHistoryController.cls is likely producing a Cartesian-style result set that is being iterated without deduplication in the LWC JavaScript controller.

## Acceptance Criteria

As per Description. Fix must ensure Coverage_Period__c records are rendered exactly once in the timeline regardless of the number or type of Rider__c child records. Regression test must cover policies with 0, 1, and 5+ COB riders.

## Solution

Fix CoverageHistoryController.cls to use a Map<Id, Coverage_Period__c> keyed on Coverage_Period__c.Id to deduplicate results before returning the list to the LWC. Refactor the SOQL to use a parent-child subquery and aggregate riders into a nested list per coverage period in Apex rather than relying on the LWC to group records. Add a Jest unit test in coverageHistoryTimeline.test.js asserting unique node count equals unique Coverage_Period__c count. Deploy hotfix to Full Sandbox for QA validation before promoting to Production.
